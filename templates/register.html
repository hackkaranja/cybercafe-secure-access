Privacy Policy

How CyberCafe Secure Access handles data

1. Information We Collect

CyberCafe Secure Access may store staff account details such as full name, username, email address, password hash, and role. The system may also store customer record information entered by staff, including customer name, email, phone number, and assigned terminal label.

For security and administration, the system may also record event data such as login attempts, timestamps, IP addresses, user activity details, and related audit information.

2. How Information Is Used

Collected data is used to authenticate users, manage customer records, monitor security activity, generate operational reports, and support accountability in shared public computer environments.

3. Security Measures

  • Passwords are stored as hashes rather than plain text.
  • Authenticated access is required before protected areas can be used.
  • CSRF protection helps guard sensitive form submissions.
  • Sessions expire automatically after inactivity.
  • Important actions are logged for review and incident response.
  • Customer email addresses may be masked in key interface views.

4. Data Sharing and Retention

Information stored by the system is intended for internal cybercafe administration and security operations. Customer records, account details, and audit logs may be retained for operational, reporting, and security purposes in line with local policy or legal requirements.

5. User Responsibility and Updates

Staff and administrators are responsible for keeping credentials secure and using the system in ways that protect customer privacy. This policy may be updated as the system changes or new legal and security requirements apply.

Rules for using CyberCafe Secure Access

1. Acceptance of Terms

By creating an account or using CyberCafe Secure Access, the user confirms that they have read, understood, and agreed to these Terms and Conditions together with the Privacy Policy presented by the system.

2. Authorized Access

Only approved staff and administrators may access the system. Users must not share credentials, attempt to access another user's account, or use the system for unauthorized monitoring or record manipulation.

3. Proper Use of Records

Customer information entered into the system must be used only for legitimate operational, administrative, and security purposes. Users must avoid misuse, unnecessary disclosure, or unauthorized copying of stored data.

4. Security Responsibilities

  • Keep login details confidential and secure.
  • Log out after using shared or public terminals.
  • Use accurate information when creating or updating records.
  • Report suspected misuse, compromise, or security issues promptly.
  • Respect role-based access limits enforced by the system.

5. Monitoring, Restrictions, and Updates

The system may record user actions, login activity, timestamps, and related audit data for security and accountability. Accounts may be restricted where misuse or security concerns are detected, and these terms may be updated as operational needs or legal requirements change.
